## Why is this research being conducted?

AI coding assistants  –  tools that write code for you as you guide and review  –  have become widely used among software developers. "Vibe coding," as it is increasingly called, raises an important question: if AI generates most of your code day-to-day, does your ability to code without it change over time?

This study aims to find out. We are recruiting Python coders across all levels of AI tool use (from none to heavy) to complete periodic Python coding challenges over 12 months. By comparing performance over time with self-reported AI coding habits, we hope to establish whether  –  and for whom  –  heavy AI-assisted coding is associated with changes in coding skill.

This is a citizen-science project: the data you contribute will help answer a question that matters to the developer community, and we will share the results  –  and the anonymised dataset  –  openly.

## Why have I been invited to take part?

You have been invited because you write code in Python. We are recruiting adults (aged 18 or over) with any level of Python experience  –  from beginners to experts, and from people who never use AI coding tools to those who use them constantly. We aim to recruit a minimum of 200 participants, with no upper limit.

You do not need to use AI coding tools to participate. We want to hear from the full spectrum.

## Do I have to take part?

No. It is entirely up to you whether you take part. You can choose not to participate without any consequences and without needing to give a reason.

If you decide to take part, you will be asked to give your consent digitally before your first session. You can withdraw from the study at any time and for any reason, simply by using the withdrawal option in your account settings. You can also request that your data be deleted at any time **before the dataset is anonymised** (approximately 12 months after your first session). After anonymisation, it will no longer be possible to identify and remove individual data, but your data will be anonymised and only used in aggregate form.

## What will my participation involve?

**Overview:** You will create an account on our platform (agenticbrainrot.com) and complete periodic coding sessions  –  roughly once a month, at a time that suits you.

**Each session:**
1. You will be presented with up to 10 short Python coding challenges, shown one at a time. Each challenge involves completing a partially written Python function.
2. Your code runs directly in your browser  –  nothing is installed and your code is not sent to a server.
3. After each challenge, you will answer a brief reflection question (e.g., "Did this feel harder than usual?").
4. You choose how many challenges to complete (from 1 to 10). You can stop at any time.
5. After your last challenge for the session, you will complete a short survey (approximately 2–3 minutes) about your current coding habits  –  including, if applicable, how much of your coding time is AI-assisted.

**Session duration:** Typically 5–25 minutes depending on how many challenges you complete.

**Session frequency:** Minimum 28 days between sessions. No maximum gap  –  you can return after a month or after several months.

**Before each session**, you will see a brief reminder to work without AI assistance (no Copilot, no ChatGPT, no Googling) and in a quiet environment where possible. This is so your performance reflects your own unassisted coding ability. You will be asked to confirm you have read this reminder.

**At study exit (or after 12 months):** You will be invited to complete a short exit survey with five optional open-ended questions about your experience of any skill changes and how you attribute them. There are no right or wrong answers.

**Optional follow-up interview:** At the end of the exit survey, you will be asked whether you would be willing to be contacted about a possible follow-up interview (by video call, approximately 45–60 minutes). This is entirely optional. If you agree, your account email will be used to contact you; you can decline or withdraw your interest at any time.

## What are the possible disadvantages and risks in taking part, and how might these be mitigated?

The risks are minimal. The coding challenges involve cognitive effort and may occasionally cause mild frustration  –  this is equivalent to everyday coding tasks and well within the range of normal professional activity. You are explicitly told before each session that this is **not a test you can fail**: the goal is to track consistent patterns over time, not to judge your performance on any given day.

You may skip any individual challenge or stop your session at any point without consequence or explanation.

Your email address is the only personally identifiable data we hold. It is used solely for account authentication and, if you opt in, for session reminder emails. It is stored securely on EU-based servers and is never shared with third parties. It is removed from the dataset before any data is shared with other researchers or released publicly.

Although we take all reasonable precautions to protect your data, it is important to note that, as with any online system, no data storage can be guaranteed to be completely free from the risk of a breach. Please contact us at andy.woods@rhul.ac.uk if you have any concerns.

## Are there any benefits in taking part?

Yes. As a participant, you will:

- Have access to a **personalised dashboard** showing your performance trends over time  –  a unique window into how your own coding skill changes.
- Contribute to the first large-scale longitudinal dataset on AI coding practices and skill  –  a question that directly affects the developer community you are part of.
- Have the opportunity to see the open dataset and published findings once released.

There is no financial payment for taking part.

## What information about me will be collected, and why?

| Data | Purpose | Stored as |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Email address | Account login and optional reminders | Identifiable; stored separately from research data |
| Profile survey responses (Python experience, role, AI tools used, etc.) | Baseline covariates for analysis | Pseudonymous (participant ID only) |
| Coding challenge performance (accuracy, active time) | Primary research outcome | Pseudonymous |
| Post-session survey responses (AI coding habits, hours per week) | Primary independent variables | Pseudonymous |
| Exit survey free-text responses | Qualitative strand | Pseudonymous; reviewed before release |
| Optional: interview recording and transcript | Qualitative strand | Identifiable during processing; recordings deleted after transcription; transcripts pseudonymised |

**Storage:** All data is stored on EU-based servers (Hetzner, Germany) in compliance with UK GDPR. The researcher (Andy Woods) and authorised members of the research team at Royal Holloway will have access to the data.

**Retention:** Email addresses and identifiable account data are deleted within 12 months of your withdrawal or the end of the study (whichever comes first). Pseudonymous research data is retained for 10 years after publication, in line with Royal Holloway data retention policy.

**Data sharing:** A fully anonymised version of the dataset will be released publicly after a 12-month embargo from the date of first data collection. Free-text responses will be reviewed for identifiability before release. Interview transcripts will never be included in the public dataset.

**Re-use:** The anonymised dataset may be used by Royal Holloway researchers and deposited in open data archives. Results may be used in academic publications, conference presentations, and other research outputs.
